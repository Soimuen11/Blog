My Dearest Mad-Readers,

I recently discovered an intriguing program: Anarchy. It is not a GNU/Linux distribution, as some might think when visiting its official website. It is an installer program in the same sense as Calameres, except that it is terminal-based (TUI). Most of it is written in JavaScript and Bash. People often complain about the difficulty of installing a distribution such as Arch Linux. Their suffering is over, my friends, for this installer has been designed to be pain-free!

As Usual...

Are you afraid of changing your habits? You should not be. The process of installing Arch on physical hardware and in a Virtual Machine is hardly any different whether you use Anarchy Linux or not.

If installing on physical hardware:

  • Download the ISO from its official web page.
  • Burn it on a USB flash drive
  • Insert said flash drive after properly configuring your BIOS or UEFI
  • Proceed with installation

For a Virtual Machine:

  • Download the ISO from its official web page.
  • Configure your virtual machine
  • Proceed with installation
